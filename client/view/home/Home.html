The Revelation Project

By Biblical Blueprints

The book of Revelation has fascinated, mystified, and terrified Christians for hundreds of years. Dr. Phil Kayser's Revelation Project seeks to clear away the smoke and present the book as John's original audience would have understood it. The 1st century Christians John wrote to knew that most of the events prophesied in Revelation would take place within their lifetime (between 66-73 AD) John filled the book with prophetic symbols about Rome, Jerusalem, and the times in which they lived — symbols from their common cultural background which he knew they would understand. Dr. Kayser makes these symbols understandable once again, as he draws from Old Testament symbology, 1st century historical works, ancient near-east archaeology, and over 100 biblical commentaries.

After studying Revelation for over thirty years, examining the best of every school of interpretation, Dr. Kayser unfolds Revelation not as an end-times-guide, but as a hope-filled practical manual on spiritual warfare, worship, prayer, perseverance in trial, resistance to tyranny, God's preservation of the church, and Jesus' victory over all of his enemies. The first fourteen sermons are useful as a primer on hermeneutics in their own right. Dr. Kayser unpacks the interpretive principles buried in the first chapter of Revelation which explain the rest of the book.

Pickering's Majority Text Translation of Revelation

Revelation has more textual variants (and thus translational differences) than any other book of the Bible. Rather than constantly discussing the technical issues of textual criticism and reasons why we differ from various translations, we chose to use Wilbur Pickering's translation of Revelation. It is based on Family 35 of the Byzantine text-type, which Dr. Kayser and others consider to be the best representative of the Majority Text, which was preserved by the church. Sermon text and audio recordings are aligned next to the relevant verses.
